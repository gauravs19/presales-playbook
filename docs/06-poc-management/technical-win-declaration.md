# Declaring the Technical Win

The Technical Win is the moment the prospect's technical evaluator formally acknowledges that your platform meets all of the pre-defined success criteria. It is the most important milestone in the presales cycle because it is the bridge between technical validation and commercial commitment.

A Technical Win that is not explicitly declared is worthless. The SE must force a binary verdict.

---

## What a Technical Win Is NOT

### It Is Not "They Liked the Demo"
A positive demo gets you a meeting. A Technical Win gets you a contract.

### It Is Not "They Said Nice Things"
"Impressive platform" and "Really slick interface" are compliments, not commitments. A Technical Win requires the prospect to say: "Yes, this meets our requirements and we are ready to move to commercial discussions."

### It Is Not Implied
If the prospect completes the POC and says nothing negative, you do not have a Technical Win. Silence is not a verdict.

## How to Force the Declaration

At the conclusion of the POC, schedule a formal "Technical Review" meeting. The attendees must include:
*   The prospect's technical evaluator (minimum)
*   The prospect's technical evaluator's manager (ideal)
*   The AE
*   The SE

### The Script

Walk through the Pass/Fail Scorecard line by line. After confirming each criterion passed, ask the decisive question:

> *"Based on the results your team has recorded across all [5] success criteria, do we have your team's recommendation to move forward? In other wordsâ€”do we have a Technical Win?"*

If they say **Yes:** Immediately transition to commercial next steps. The AE takes over. The SE schedules the internal handoff debrief.

If they say **"Yes, but...":** Probe the "but." There is a residual concern. Address it on the spot or schedule a focused follow-up within 48 hours. Do not let the "but" go unresolved.

If they say **"We need more time":** This is not a neutral response. It is a soft No. Ask: "What specific criteria remain unproven, and what would it take to prove them in the next 5 business days?" If they cannot answer specifically, the Technical Win is in serious jeopardy.
