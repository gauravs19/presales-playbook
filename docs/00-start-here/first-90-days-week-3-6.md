1. **Shadow with a specific focus per call.** Don't just sit on calls passively. Before each shadowed meeting, pick one thing to observe. Monday: watch how the senior SE opens a discovery call. 2. **Run the "invisible debrief."** After every shadowed call, spend 10 minutes writing down: What did the SE do well? What would I have done differently? What question did the prospect ask that I couldn't have answered? Share your debrief notes with the senior SE and ask for their reaction. This single practice will accelerate your ramp by 40% because it forces you to engage analytically with what you observed, not just absorb it passively.

3. **Take your first "section" of a call.** Around week 4, ask the senior SE if you can run one specific section of a customer call â€” typically the product overview or a single use-case walkthrough. 4. **Build your first persona-mapped demo narrative.** Take one of the top 5 use cases and build a 15-minute demo script around a specific persona. Not "here's how feature X works." Instead: "You're a data engineering manager at a mid-market fintech. Your team spends 3 hours every morning manually reconciling yesterday's transactions. Here's how that changes." Personas make features stick. Practice this narrative until you can deliver it without notes.

> **War Story:** A new SE shadowed a senior colleague on a complex discovery call. The prospect's lead architect asked about database failover behaviour during a regional outage. > **Failure Mode:** The new SE who shadows for 6 weeks and never debriefs. **Interview Angle:**
"Tell me about a time you learned a critical skill by observing another SE. What did you observe and what did you take from it?"
A strong answer identifies a specific behaviour or decision, explains the reasoning behind it, and describes how it changed their own approach. A weak answer says "I learned a lot from watching senior people."
