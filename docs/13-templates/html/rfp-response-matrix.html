RFP Response Matrix

Part A decides whether to respond at all — before anyone writes a word. Part B makes every "yes" strategic. Most RFP losses happen at the decision to respond, not in the response.

PurposeConvert sunk-cost reflex into a scored bid decision; ban the naked "yes"
When to usePart A within 48h of RFP receipt; Part B only if A says respond
OwnerSE (technical scoring) + AE (relationship scoring)
Time & outputA: ~25 min · B: ongoing · to the bid/no-bid meeting, then the proposal team

How to use this document

  • Part A is a gate, not a formality. If you've never scored below the respond threshold, you're rubber-stamping. The RFPs you decline fund the effort on the ones you can win.
  • Run the fingerprint check first. An RFP written around a competitor's spec sheet is their deal with your name on the distribution list — respond only to reposition, or not at all.
  • Every "yes" carries a mechanism, timeframe, or proof point. Unqualified yeses compound into an implementation gap delivery inherits.
  • Track the No percentage with pride. A 95%-"Full" response reads as a miracle or a lie — honest No's with alternatives make your yeses believable.
