Stage 05 · Prove

Technical Win Report

The formal declaration that converts a finished POC into commercial momentum — results vs. signed criteria, stakeholder verdicts in their own words, and the next step their signature already committed to.

PurposeDeclare the win in writing before its half-life decays; force the committed next step
When to useWithin 3 business days of the POC end date
OwnerSE
Time & output~20 min · to eval lead + exec sponsor, AE, your leadership

How to use this document

  • Collect the verdicts BEFORE writing. Ask each evaluator "in your own words, did this meet the bar?" while the wins are fresh — their quotes close deals in rooms you'll never enter.
  • Quote the signed plan verbatim. Criteria and the §1 commitment are copy-pasted, never paraphrased. Nobody argues with their own signature.
  • Put the caveats in yourself. A report with zero caveats reads as marketing and invites their engineers to supply the caveats for you — in a meeting you're not in.
  • Send it up, not just across. The eval lead knows you won; this document exists for the economic buyer and procurement, who only ever see artifacts.

  • Attach evidence to every criterion row

Don't
  • Wait for the prospect to "socialise it internally"

Before this report ships

  • Verdicts collected verbally and quoted with permission
  • Criteria text matches the signed plan word-for-word
  • Caveats section has at least one honest entry
  • Next-step date proposed, AE aligned on the follow-through
