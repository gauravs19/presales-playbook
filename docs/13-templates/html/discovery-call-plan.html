Stage 02 · Discover

Discovery Call Plan

Research, hypothesis, role split, and question arc — agreed with the AE before the call, so two professionals never improvise in front of a prospect.

PurposeWalk in with a testable hypothesis and an agreed plan, not a blank page
When to useThe day before any discovery call; reviewed with the AE 5 min before dial-in
OwnerSE (research & arc) · shared with AE
Time & output~20 min research + 5 min alignment · feeds the Discovery Summary

How to use this document

  • Research earns the right to ask. Your first question should prove you did homework — it changes how every later answer is given.
  • Write the hypothesis so the prospect can say "no, that's wrong." Their correction is the most valuable data you'll collect all call.
  • Six questions maximum. Twenty questions is an interrogation plan. Follow-ups come from listening, not the list.
  • The plan is a compass, not a script. Answer detours briefly, then return: "Happy to go deep there — first, can I test one assumption?"

7 · Success Criteria for THIS Call

  • Pain quantified in their numbers, not our slides
  • Economic buyer named, or a path to them identified
  • Concrete next step accepted, with a date on the calendar
Do
  • Share the hypothesis with the AE before the call
  • Let silence work after your best questions
  • Capture verbatim quotes — they power every later artifact
Don't
  • Open with a company overview — start with a question
  • Run full discovery on a call that's clearly a non-deal
  • Answer pricing; that lane belongs to the AE

Five minutes before dial-in (with the AE)

  • Objective confirmed in one sentence
  • Roles & minute-map agreed; interruption/walk-away signals set
  • Hypothesis shared — AE confirms or corrects with their intel
  • Next-step proposal scripted verbatim
