Stage 04 · Demo

Demo Storyboard

Scene-by-scene narrative plan mapping each capability to a discovered pain — with tension/relief beats, persona bindings, and the pre-flight checklist. The antidote to the harbor tour.

PurposeDesign the demo as a story the audience retells, not a feature inventory
When to use2–3 days before any tailored demo — never for one without discovery behind it
OwnerSE · scene table optionally doubles as the meeting agenda
Time & output~30 min · self + AE; feeds post-demo capture

How to use this document

  • Scenes, not features. Each row is a moment in their workday — "Priya's Monday-morning pipeline triage" — never a module name. Can't name the persona living in the scene? Cut the scene.
  • The aha is a design constraint, not a hope. Decide what it is, then clear everything in front of it. An aha at minute 20 plays to a room that has already left.
  • Section 4 is where discipline lives. Every capability you cut is one that can't dilute the message.
  • Rehearse the crash, not just the demo. You don't choose whether the environment fails — only whether the recovery is rehearsed.

Do
  • Open with their pain in their words
  • Make the champion's persona the hero of a scene
  • End with a scripted, dated next-step ask
Don't
  • Tour the product "for context" before the first scene
  • Show anything that doesn't map to a discovered pain
  • Let a question hijack the arc — answer, bridge, return

Storyboard quality gate

  • Every scene names a persona and a discovered pain
  • Aha lands before minute 5
  • Cut-list (section 4) has at least two entries — if it's empty, you haven't chosen
  • Objection responses drafted, not improvised
