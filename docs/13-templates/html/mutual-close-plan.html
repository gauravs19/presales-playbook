Stage 07 · Close

Mutual Close Plan

A shared, dated, named-owner timeline from today to go-live, built backwards from the prospect's own deadline. Deals die in the ninety days between "we choose you" and a signature — in security reviews nobody scheduled and legal queues nobody owned.

PurposeMake the close a jointly managed project instead of a hope
When to useThe moment a technical win is declared — before commercial negotiation starts
OwnerAE drives · SE owns technical & security gates
Time & output~25 min draft + 1 champion call · to champion, procurement, both sponsors; reviewed weekly

How to use this document

  • Anchor to their date, never yours. Built backwards from their fiscal event or launch, every slip threatens their own commitment — and their team starts pushing procurement for you.
  • Ask the queue-time questions out loud, in week one. "How long does your security review typically take?" asked early is a planning input; discovered in week nine, it's a lost quarter.
  • One name per gate, both sides. "Their legal team" is not an owner. Department names instead of people = a wish, and everyone knows it.
  • The champion must co-author it. Every edit they make converts your timeline into their commitment.

The Sentence That Makes It Mutual

"You told us by matters because . This plan is the only path that gets there — where do you see risk in it?"
Do
  • Hand the champion the marker — their edits are commitment
  • Re-date visibly when something slips
  • Keep the weekly check to ten minutes, every week
Don't
  • Build it around your quarter-end
  • Accept "their legal team" as an owner
  • Write it once and never mention it again

Plan quality gate

  • Anchored to a real prospect-side date with a stated reason
  • Every gate has one named owner per side and a date
  • Friction questions asked and answered, not assumed
  • Champion has edited the document with their own hands
